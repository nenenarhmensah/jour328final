Maryland Education Bills Passed by the General Assembly in 2023

Education has been one of the most popular news topics of the past year and the Maryland Legislature reflected that shift. Education was the fifth most popular legislation topic of the session, 58 or the 810 bills passed were related to education. The legislative session wrapped up a few weeks ago and the final day for an extended session is May 10. Here's a detailed breakdown on the education bills passed.

Gov. Wes Moore was the primary sponsor of two of the bills that passed this session. Both were key policies he promised to enact during his term. Senate Bill 0551 laid the groundwork for the a service year program for young adults and House Bill 1219 which required the State Department of Education to create goals for teacher recruitment and retention.

Most of the legislation passed contained language about altering current policies. Many of the bills passed also changed the requirements of certain organizations including higher education institutions and the State Department of Education.
